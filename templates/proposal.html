Our Proposal For Fighting Voter Suppression

What is voter supression?

‘Demand the Vote’ defines voter suppression as “any effort, either legal or illegal, by way of laws, administrative rules, and/or tactics that prevent eligible voters from registering to vote or voting.” Voter suppression is exemplified through the following:
  • Forcing racist and discriminatory voter photo-ID and citizenship identification checks
  • Eliminating voter opportunities by reducing the hours at polling stations in communities of color

Who is affected by voter suppression?

Voter suppression most commonly targets black voters and other communities of color. The American Civil Liberties Union writes that “since 2008, states across the country have passed measures to make it harder for Americans—particularly black people, the elderly, students, and people with disabilities—to exercise their fundamental right to cast a ballot.”

Who benefits from voter suppression?

Republicans. It is clear that voter suppression laws and tactics benefits those who propose them -- Republic federal and state lawmakers.

OUR PROPOSAL

National Voting Day (a red, white and blue holiday)! In order to increase voter turnout and reduce voter suppression, we propose a national holiday to ensure all people regardless of their skin color, socioeconomic status, and ability have the opportunity to exercise their right to vote.
